2.Understanding sin and cos as Lengths

You probably know about sine and cosine, which have to do with triangles. But when the longest side(Hypotenuse) of a triangle is just 1 unit long, something interesting happens.

3.Pictures to Help

Imagine a Right-triangle where one of the angles is (x+y). On the other side, there are two Right-triangles, one on top of the other, with angles x and y. This arrangement helps us see how sin(x+y) is connected to sin(x)cos(y) and cos(x)sin(y).
